## ✅ Answers and Explanations

---

**Q1. Correct Answer: D — Third Party Audit**

> A Third Party Audit is conducted by an **independent external
> body** — such as a certification body or regulator. First Party =
> organization audits itself (internal audit). Second Party =
> customer audits its supplier. Third Party = independent body
> like ISO certification authority — no relationship to either party.

---

**Q2. Correct Answer: B — Planning → Fieldwork → Analysis →
Reporting → Follow-up**

> The correct sequence: **Planning** (define scope) → **Fieldwork**
> (collect evidence) → **Analysis** (evaluate findings) →
> **Reporting** (document results) → **Follow-up** (verify fixes).
> Option A starts with Fieldwork — skipping Planning. Option C
> swaps Analysis and Fieldwork. Option D places Reporting before
> Analysis — impossible since you need analysis before reporting.

---

**Q3. Correct Answer: D — EAL4**

> **EAL4 = Methodically Designed, Tested and Reviewed** — the
> most common commercial evaluation level. It is the highest EAL
> achievable without fundamentally redesigning a product for
> evaluation. Most enterprise firewalls, operating systems, and
> security products target EAL4. EAL5–EAL7 are for specialized
> high-security environments.

---

**Q4. Correct Answer: C — A community or industry group defining
generic requirements**

> A **Protection Profile (PP)** is created by a **community or
> industry group** — it defines generic security requirements for
> a category of products (e.g., all firewalls). A **Security Target
> (ST)** is created by the developer for their specific product.
> The CCTL evaluates. The Certification Body certifies. PP = generic
> template / ST = product-specific claims.

---

**Q5. Correct Answer: C — Verified Design — the highest level**

> **TCSEC Class A1 = Verified Design** — the highest level requiring
> formal mathematical verification of the design. The order from
> lowest to highest is: D (Minimal) → C1 → C2 → B1 → B2 → B3 →
> A1. Class D = Minimal Protection (lowest). C2 = Controlled Access.
> B2 = Structured Protection.

---

**Q6. Correct Answer: B — Executive Order 13636**

> **Executive Order 13636** — "Improving Critical Infrastructure
> Cybersecurity" — signed in February 2013 directed NIST to develop
> a cybersecurity framework, resulting in CSF 1.0 published in
> February 2014. EO 13800 = a 2017 executive order on cybersecurity.
> EO 14028 = 2021 order on improving national cybersecurity.

---

**Q7. Correct Answer: D — Adaptive**

> **Tier 4 = Adaptive** — the highest tier, characterized by
> proactive adaptation to threats and continuous improvement
> organization-wide. Tier 1 = Partial (ad hoc). Tier 2 = Risk
> Informed (risk awareness exists but informal). Tier 3 = Repeatable
> (formally approved policies consistently applied). Tier 4 =
> Adaptive (proactive and continuously improving).

---

**Q8. Correct Answer: C — Risk Management Framework (RMF)**

> **NIST SP 800-37 = Risk Management Framework (RMF)** — the
> structured process for integrating security into the system
> development lifecycle. It has 7 steps: Prepare, Categorize,
> Select, Implement, Assess, Authorize, Monitor. SP 800-53 =
> Security and Privacy Controls catalog. The CSF is defined in
> a separate NIST publication — not an SP 800 number.

---

**Q9. Correct Answer: D — Genetic data**

> **Genetic data** is one of the 8 Special Categories under
> GDPR Article 9 — requiring extra protection with a general
> prohibition on processing unless specific exceptions apply.
> Email address, home address, and financial transaction records
> are personal data but NOT special categories. The 8 special
> categories include: racial/ethnic origin, political opinions,
> religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data,
> biometric data (for ID), health data, sex life/orientation.

---

**Q10. Correct Answer: C — 72 hours**

> **GDPR requires DPA notification within 72 hours** of becoming
> aware of a breach — where it is likely to result in risk to
> individuals' rights and freedoms. Individual notification must
> happen "without undue delay" only if HIGH risk. 24 hours is
> not a GDPR requirement. 60 days = HIPAA timeline.
> 72 hours = GDPR's most tested number.

---

**Q11. Correct Answer: C — 8**

> GDPR gives data subjects exactly **8 rights**: Right to be
> Informed, Access, Rectification, Erasure (Right to be Forgotten),
> Restrict Processing, Data Portability, Object, and Rights regarding
> Automated Decision Making. DPDPA 2023 has only 4 rights.
> The number 8 is directly tested alongside DPDPA's 4 rights.

---

**Q12. Correct Answer: B — 14 domains — 114 controls**

> **ISO 27001:2013 = 14 domains, 114 controls.** The three
> version facts: 2005 (11 domains, 133 controls) → 2013 (14
> domains, 114 controls) → 2022 (4 themes, 93 controls). Option A
> = 2005 version facts. Option C = 2022 version facts. Option D
> is fictional.

---

**Q13. Correct Answer: C — Clause 6.1.3**

> **Clause 6.1.3** — Information Security Risk Treatment —
> requires production of a **Statement of Applicability (SoA)**
> listing all Annex A controls with inclusion/exclusion status
> and justification. Clause 4.3 = ISMS scope. Clause 5.1 = top
> management commitment. Clause 9.2 = internal audit requirements.

---

**Q14. Correct Answer: C — 27001 contains requirements — 27002
provides implementation guidance**

> **ISO 27001 = requirements (WHAT to do)** — the certifiable
> standard. **ISO 27002 = guidance (HOW to do it)** — detailed
> implementation guidance for each Annex A control. You get
> certified to 27001. You USE 27002 as a reference for implementing
> controls. 27002 is NOT certifiable. Neither replaced the other
> in 2022 — both were updated simultaneously.

---

**Q15. Correct Answer: C — Assessment and reporting on
effectiveness of ICFR**

> **SOX Section 404** = management must assess and report on the
> effectiveness of **Internal Controls over Financial Reporting
> (ICFR)** — using the COSO framework. Section 302 = CEO/CFO
> personal certification of financial statements. Section 409 =
> real-time disclosure. Section 802 = criminal penalties for
> document destruction.

---

**Q16. Correct Answer: D — Security**

> **Security is the ONLY mandatory Trust Services Criteria** in
> SOC 2. All other four — Availability, Processing Integrity,
> Confidentiality, and Privacy — are optional based on the nature
> of the service. A SOC 2 report MUST include Security. This is
> one of the most frequently tested SOC 2 facts.

---

**Q17. Correct Answer: B — Tests design AND operating effectiveness
over a minimum 6-month period**

> **Type II tests BOTH design AND operating effectiveness** over
> a **minimum 6-month period** — proving controls actually worked
> consistently over time. Type I tests only whether controls are
> suitably designed at a single point in time. Both are conducted
> by external CPA firms. Both SOC 2 types are restricted reports —
> neither is public (SOC 3 is public).

---

**Q18. Correct Answer: B — Added the EDM domain and separated
Governance from Management**

> **COBIT 5 (2012)** formally separated Governance from Management
> by introducing the **EDM (Evaluate, Direct, Monitor)** domain
> as the governance domain. COBIT 4.1 had 4 domains. COBIT 5
> expanded to 5 domains — adding EDM at the governance level.
> Implementation Groups belong to CIS Controls — not COBIT.

---

**Q19. Correct Answer: D — EDM**

> **EDM = Evaluate, Direct, and Monitor** — the GOVERNANCE domain
> in COBIT 5 and 2019 — operating at the board/ownership level.
> APO (Align, Plan, Organise), BAI (Build, Acquire, Implement),
> DSS (Deliver, Service, Support) and MEA (Monitor, Evaluate, Assess)
> are all MANAGEMENT domains operating at the executive/operational
> level.

---

**Q20. Correct Answer: C — 40**

> **COBIT 2019 = 40 governance and management objectives** — up
> from 37 processes in COBIT 5. COBIT 4.1 = 34 processes. COBIT
> 2019 also renamed "processes" to "governance and management
> objectives" reflecting a more outcome-focused approach. The
> 5-domain structure (EDM, APO, BAI, DSS, MEA) remained the same.

---

**Q21. Correct Answer: C — Administrative Simplification —
Privacy and Security Rules**

> **HIPAA Title II = Preventing Health Care Fraud and Administrative
> Simplification** — the most important title for IT and security
> professionals. It contains the Privacy Rule, Security Rule,
> Breach Notification Rule, and Enforcement Rule. Title I =
> insurance portability. Title III = tax provisions. Title IV =
> group health plan requirements.

---

**Q22. Correct Answer: D — 60 days**

> **HIPAA breach notification = 60 days** from discovery — to
> notify affected individuals and HHS. This is a critical
> distinction from GDPR (72 hours to DPA). Media notification
> is also required within 60 days if 500+ individuals in a single
> state are affected. For small breaches (<500 individuals),
> HHS is notified within 60 days after the calendar year end.

---

**Q23. Correct Answer: C — 18**

> HIPAA Safe Harbor de-identification requires removal of exactly
> **18 specified identifiers** including names, geographic data,
> dates (except year), phone numbers, SSN, email addresses,
> IP addresses, biometric identifiers, and more. ALL 18 must be
> removed. Partial removal does NOT de-identify the data.
> The alternative method is Expert Determination.

---

**Q24. Correct Answer: C — A private industry standard enforced
contractually**

> **PCI DSS is a private industry standard** — NOT a government
> law like HIPAA, NOT an EU regulation like GDPR, and NOT a
> voluntary standard like ISO 27001. It is enforced through
> contractual agreements between merchants, acquirers, and payment
> card brands (Visa, Mastercard, etc.). Non-compliance can result
> in loss of card processing privileges.

---

**Q25. Correct Answer: C — 6**

> PCI DSS has **12 requirements organized under 6 goals**:
> Goal 1 (Req 1–2): Build secure network / Goal 2 (Req 3–4):
> Protect data / Goal 3 (Req 5–6): Vulnerability management /
> Goal 4 (Req 7–8–9): Access control / Goal 5 (Req 10–11):
> Monitor and test / Goal 6 (Req 12): Security policy.
> Both numbers — 12 requirements AND 6 goals — are tested together.

---

**Q26. Correct Answer: D — AOC — Attestation of Compliance**

> The **AOC (Attestation of Compliance)** is required by ALL
> merchants and service providers — it is the summary sign-off
> document confirming SAQ or ROC results. The ROC is only required
> for Level 1 merchants and Level 1 service providers. The SAQ
> is used by Level 2–4 merchants and Level 2 service providers.
> Everyone needs an AOC — not everyone needs a ROC or SAQ.

---

**Q27. Correct Answer: C — SANS Institute — originally called
SANS Top 20**

> CIS Controls were **originally developed by the SANS Institute**
> in 2008 as the **"SANS Top 20"** (Twenty Critical Security Controls).
> Stewardship transferred to Council on Cybersecurity (2011) and
> then to Center for Internet Security — CIS (2015). They were
> rebranded as CIS Critical Security Controls. ISO, ISACA, and
> NIST had no role in creating them.

---

**Q28. Correct Answer: B — Essential security baseline appropriate
for all organizations**

> **CIS Benchmark Level 1 = essential security baseline** —
> practical, prudent recommendations appropriate for ALL
> organizations with minimal impact on system functionality.
> Level 2 = advanced security for high-security environments
> that may impact functionality. Neither level is a mandatory
> government standard or a certification. Level 1 first —
> then assess Level 2.

---

**Q29. Correct Answer: D — NSA — National Security Agency**

> The **NSA (National Security Agency)** initiated the SSE-CMM
> research project in 1993 — driven by US government and defense
> needs for a standard to measure security engineering capability.
> CISA was established much later (2018). NIST develops SP 800
> series but did not initiate SSE-CMM. DHS had no role.

---

**Q30. Correct Answer: C — Domain Dimension — defining WHAT
activities are performed**

> **Base Practices (BPs) belong to the Domain Dimension** —
> they are specific activities within each Process Area defining
> WHAT must be done in security engineering. **Generic Practices
> (GPs) belong to the Capability Dimension** — they define HOW
> WELL processes are performed across all Process Areas.
> The two dimensions create the SSE-CMM matrix.

---

**Q31. Correct Answer: B — Base Practices are performed but
informally — ad hoc and dependent on individual skill**

> **Level 1 = Performed Informally** — Base Practices ARE being
> performed but execution is reactive, ad hoc, and depends on
> individual knowledge ("individual heroics"). There is NO formal
> planning or tracking. Level 1 is NOT "doing nothing" — it IS
> performing security engineering, just without process discipline.
> Level 0 does not exist in SSE-CMM.

---

**Q32. Correct Answer: D — Body corporates that possess or handle
SPDI**

> **Section 43A** applies to **body corporates** (companies and
> organizations) that possess, deal with, or handle **Sensitive
> Personal Data or Information (SPDI)**. They must maintain
> reasonable security practices. Negligent failure to do so makes
> them liable to pay compensation. It does NOT apply only to
> government agencies or healthcare organizations.

---

**Q33. Correct Answer: C — CERT-In — Indian Computer Emergency
Response Team**

> **Section 70B of ITAA 2008 formally established CERT-In** as
> India's national nodal agency for cybersecurity incident response.
> MeitY is the administering ministry — not established by 70B.
> NIC is a separate government IT organization. DPBI was created
> much later by DPDPA 2023.

---

**Q34. Correct Answer: B — Not covered — DPDPA applies to digital
personal data only**

> **DPDPA 2023 applies ONLY to digital personal data** — paper
> and physical records are excluded. This is a significant
> difference from GDPR which covers ALL forms of personal data
> processing including paper records. A paper patient record is
> NOT covered by DPDPA. The same information in an electronic
> health record IS covered.

---

**Q35. Correct Answer: C — 4 rights**

> **DPDPA 2023 grants 4 rights** to Data Principals: Right to
> Access Information, Right to Correction and Erasure, Right to
> Grievance Redressal, and Right to Nominate. This is significantly
> fewer than GDPR's 8 rights. Absent from DPDPA: Right to Data
> Portability, Right to Object, Right to Restrict Processing,
> and Automated Decision-Making rights.

---

**Q36. Correct Answer: D — A registered entity providing a single
platform for Data Principals to manage consent**

> The **Consent Manager** is a **DPDPA-unique concept** — a
> registered entity (registered with DPBI) that provides a single
> interoperable platform through which Data Principals can give,
> manage, review, and withdraw consent across multiple Data
> Fiduciaries. There is **no equivalent concept in GDPR** —
> making it one of the most distinctive aspects of DPDPA 2023.

---

**Q37. Correct Answer: B — 6 hours**

> **CERT-In's 2022 mandatory directions require reporting within
> 6 hours** of noticing a cybersecurity incident — the strictest
> mandatory reporting timeline among major frameworks. Compare:
> GDPR = 72 hours (to DPA), HIPAA = 60 days (to individuals/HHS).
> India's CERT-In requirement is far stricter than both GDPR and
> HIPAA on incident reporting timeline.

---

**Q38. Correct Answer: C — Covers commercial bribery between
private parties and prohibits facilitation payments**

> **UKBA is stricter than FCPA because:**
> 1. UKBA covers **ALL bribery** — including commercial bribery
> between private parties. FCPA covers only foreign government
> official bribery.
> 2. UKBA **prohibits facilitation payments** entirely. FCPA has
> a narrow exception allowing them.
> ISO 37001 certification is not mandated by either law — though
> it provides a "adequate procedures" defense under UKBA.

---

**Q39. Correct Answer: D — Segregation of Duties (SOD)**

> **Segregation of Duties (SOD)** is the control that prevents
> a single employee from having end-to-end control over a financial
> process — specifically preventing the same person from both
> initiating AND approving a payment. A whistleblower hotline
> provides anonymous reporting. Gifts register tracks hospitality.
> Third-party due diligence screens vendors. Only SOD directly
> prevents single-person payment fraud.

---

**Q40. Correct Answer: C — SEC — Securities and Exchange
Commission**

> **The SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission) enforces SOX**
> — it issues rules, regulations, and enforcement actions under
> the Sarbanes-Oxley Act. **PCAOB** was created BY SOX to oversee
> auditors of public companies — but PCAOB reports to the SEC and
> does not enforce SOX directly. AICPA governs the SOC reporting
> framework. NIST has no SOX enforcement role.
