## MCQ Set

**Q1. COBIT stands for and is developed by which organization?**

A) Control Objectives for IT and Business — developed by ISO
B) Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies —
   developed by ISACA ✅
C) Compliance Objectives for Information Technology — developed by NIST
D) Control Objectives for Information Technology — developed by AICPA

> **Explanation:** **COBIT = Control Objectives for Information and
> Related Technologies** — developed and maintained by **ISACA
> (Information Systems Audit and Control Association)**. ISO develops
> international standards (like ISO 27001). NIST develops US frameworks
> (like CSF). AICPA created the SOC reporting framework. Knowing both
> the full name AND the developing organization is directly tested.

---

**Q2. COBIT is primarily classified as which type of framework?**

A) Information security management system standard
B) IT service management framework
C) IT governance and management framework ✅
D) Software development lifecycle framework

> **Explanation:** COBIT is an **IT governance and management framework**
> — its primary focus is ensuring IT is aligned with business goals,
> delivers value, manages risk, and optimizes resources. It is NOT an
> information security standard (that is ISO 27001), NOT an IT service
> management framework (that is ITIL), and NOT a software development
> framework.

---

**Q3. COBIT was first published in which year and by which
organization?**

A) 1992 — AICPA
B) 1995 — ISO/IEC
C) 1996 — ISACA ✅
D) 2000 — NIST

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 1 was first published in 1996 by ISACA**
> — originally focused on IT audit and control objectives. It has since
> evolved through multiple versions (4.1, 5, 2019). AICPA created the
> SOC framework. ISO/IEC publishes international standards. NIST
> published the CSF in 2014.

---

**Q4. COBIT 4.1 organized its framework into how many domains
containing how many processes and control objectives?**

A) 5 domains — 37 processes — 250 control objectives
B) 4 domains — 34 processes — 210 control objectives ✅
C) 4 domains — 30 processes — 180 control objectives
D) 5 domains — 40 processes — 210 control objectives

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 4.1 = 4 domains, 34 processes, 210 control
> objectives.** This is one of the three critical version facts:
> COBIT 4.1 (4 domains/34 processes/210 objectives) →
> COBIT 5 (5 domains/37 processes) →
> COBIT 2019 (5 domains/40 objectives).

---

**Q5. Which of the following correctly lists all 4 domains of
COBIT 4.1?**

A) Plan and Organise, Acquire and Implement, Deliver and Support,
   Monitor and Evaluate ✅
B) Evaluate and Direct, Align and Plan, Build and Acquire,
   Deliver and Support
C) Plan and Organise, Build and Implement, Deliver and Support,
   Monitor and Assess
D) Evaluate, Plan, Deliver, Monitor

> **Explanation:** The correct COBIT 4.1 domains are:
> **Plan and Organise (PO), Acquire and Implement (AI),
> Deliver and Support (DS), Monitor and Evaluate (ME).**
> Option B describes COBIT 5/2019 domain names. The 4.1 domains
> use different verbs — Acquire (not Build), Deliver (not DSS),
> Monitor and Evaluate (not Assess).

---

**Q6. Which COBIT version formally and explicitly separated the
concept of Governance from Management for the first time?**

A) COBIT 3
B) COBIT 4.1
C) COBIT 5 ✅
D) COBIT 2019

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 5 (2012)** was the first version to formally
> separate Governance from Management — introducing the EDM domain
> (Evaluate, Direct, Monitor) as the governance domain and four
> management domains (APO, BAI, DSS, MEA). While earlier versions
> touched on governance, COBIT 5 made the separation explicit and
> structural.

---

**Q7. In COBIT 5 and COBIT 2019, which domain operates at the
GOVERNANCE level — representing board and ownership responsibilities?**

A) APO — Align, Plan, and Organise
B) BAI — Build, Acquire, and Implement
C) EDM — Evaluate, Direct, and Monitor ✅
D) MEA — Monitor, Evaluate, and Assess

> **Explanation:** **EDM = Evaluate, Direct, and Monitor** is the
> GOVERNANCE domain — it operates at the board/ownership level and
> covers strategic direction, evaluation of options, and monitoring
> of performance. APO, BAI, DSS, and MEA are all **management**
> domains — they operate at the executive/operational level.

---

**Q8. COBIT 5 has how many domains and how many processes in total?**

A) 4 domains — 34 processes
B) 5 domains — 40 processes
C) 5 domains — 37 processes ✅
D) 6 domains — 37 processes

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 5 = 5 domains, 37 processes.**
> The 5 domains are EDM (governance) + APO, BAI, DSS, MEA
> (management). COBIT 2019 kept the same 5 domains but increased
> objectives to 40. COBIT 4.1 had 4 domains and 34 processes.

---

**Q9. COBIT 2019 updated from COBIT 5 by changing the total number
of governance and management objectives to:**

A) 34
B) 37
C) 40 ✅
D) 45

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 2019 = 40 governance and management
> objectives** — up from 37 processes in COBIT 5. COBIT 2019 also
> renamed "processes" to "governance and management objectives" to
> reflect a more outcome-focused approach. The domain structure
> (EDM, APO, BAI, DSS, MEA) remained the same.

---

**Q10. Two major additions introduced in COBIT 2019 that were NOT
present in COBIT 5 are:**

A) Governance domain and Management domain
B) Design Factors and Focus Areas ✅
C) Goals Cascade and BSC Perspectives
D) Capability Levels and Maturity Model

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 2019 added Design Factors and Focus Areas**
> — both were absent in COBIT 5. Design Factors allow organizations
> to tailor COBIT to their specific context. Focus Areas provide
> specialized guidance for topics like cybersecurity, cloud, DevOps,
> and privacy. The Governance/Management separation, Goals Cascade,
> and capability model all existed in COBIT 5.

---

**Q11. COBIT 5 was built on how many principles?**

A) 4
B) 5 ✅
C) 6
D) 7

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 5 has 5 principles.** COBIT 2019 updated
> this to 6 principles — adding "Dynamic Governance System" and
> "Tailored to Enterprise Needs." This 5 vs 6 distinction is a
> common MCQ trap — know which version has which number.

---

**Q12. Which of the following correctly lists ALL 5 principles of
COBIT 5?**

A) Provide Stakeholder Value, Holistic Approach, Dynamic Governance,
   Governance Distinct from Management, Tailored to Enterprise Needs
B) Meeting Stakeholder Needs, Covering Enterprise End-to-End,
   Applying Single Integrated Framework, Enabling Holistic Approach,
   Separating Governance from Management ✅
C) Meeting Stakeholder Needs, Risk Management, Holistic Approach,
   Separating Governance, Compliance Focus
D) Stakeholder Value, Enterprise Coverage, Single Framework,
   Dynamic Governance, Tailored Approach

> **Explanation:** The exact **5 COBIT 5 principles** are:
> 1. Meeting Stakeholder Needs
> 2. Covering the Enterprise End-to-End
> 3. Applying a Single Integrated Framework
> 4. Enabling a Holistic Approach
> 5. Separating Governance from Management
> Option A describes COBIT 2019 principles. Exact wording matters
> in MCQs.

**Q13. COBIT 2019 added two NEW principles compared to COBIT 5.
Which of the following correctly identifies both new additions?**

A) Providing Stakeholder Value and End-to-End Governance System
B) Holistic Approach and Governance Distinct from Management
C) Dynamic Governance System and Tailored to Enterprise Needs ✅
D) Single Integrated Framework and Risk-Based Approach

> **Explanation:** COBIT 2019 added exactly **two new principles**:
> **Dynamic Governance System** (recognizing governance must adapt to
> changing context) and **Tailored to Enterprise Needs** (recognizing
> no one-size-fits-all approach). The other four principles are updated
> versions of existing COBIT 5 principles — not entirely new.

---

**Q14. The COBIT Goals Cascade shows the chain from stakeholder
needs down to governance objectives. The correct order of this
cascade is:**

A) IT Goals → Enterprise Goals → Stakeholder Needs → COBIT Objectives
B) Stakeholder Needs → Alignment Goals → Enterprise Goals →
   COBIT Objectives
C) Stakeholder Needs → Enterprise Goals → Alignment Goals →
   Governance and Management Objectives ✅
D) COBIT Objectives → Enterprise Goals → Stakeholder Needs →
   IT Goals

> **Explanation:** The correct **Goals Cascade** flows from top to
> bottom:
> **Stakeholder Needs → Enterprise Goals (17 goals across 4 BSC
> perspectives) → Alignment Goals (13 IT-related goals) →
> Governance and Management Objectives (40 in COBIT 2019).**
> This cascade justifies IT governance in business terms.

---

**Q15. The Goals Cascade in COBIT uses which strategic framework's
perspectives to organize Enterprise Goals?**

A) COSO Internal Control Framework
B) Porter's Five Forces
C) Balanced Scorecard (BSC) ✅
D) SWOT Analysis

> **Explanation:** COBIT's Goals Cascade organizes Enterprise Goals
> using the **Balanced Scorecard (BSC)** perspectives: Financial,
> Customer, Internal (Process), and Learning & Growth. This connects
> IT governance to the same strategic lens that business leadership
> uses — making IT goals relevant to the board and executives.

---

**Q16. In COBIT 2019, the governance system has how many components
(formerly called "Enablers" in COBIT 5)?**

A) 5
B) 6
C) 7 ✅
D) 9

> **Explanation:** Both COBIT 5 and COBIT 2019 define **7 components**
> of the governance system (COBIT 5 called them Enablers — COBIT 2019
> renamed them Components). The 7 are: Processes, Organizational
> Structures, Principles/Policies/Frameworks, Information, Culture/
> Ethics/Behavior, People/Skills/Competencies, and Services/
> Infrastructure/Applications.

---

**Q17. The fundamental distinction between IT Governance and IT
Management in COBIT is:**

A) Governance is operational; Management is strategic
B) Governance sets direction and oversight; Management plans
   and executes ✅
C) Governance is done by IT managers; Management is done by the board
D) Governance covers security; Management covers everything else

> **Explanation:** In COBIT: **Governance = board/ownership level —
> sets strategic direction, evaluates options, monitors performance.**
> **Management = executive/operational level — plans, builds, runs,
> and monitors.** They are NOT the same and NOT interchangeable.
> The board governs; the executives manage. Confusing these is a very
> common MCQ trap.

---

**Q18. COBIT and ITIL are best described as:**

A) Competing frameworks — organizations must choose one or the other
B) Identical frameworks with different names
C) Complementary frameworks — COBIT governs, ITIL operationalizes ✅
D) Sequential frameworks — ITIL must be implemented before COBIT

> **Explanation:** **COBIT and ITIL are complementary — not
> competing.** COBIT provides IT governance context (WHAT IT should
> do and WHY). ITIL provides IT service management processes (HOW
> IT services should be delivered). Many organizations use BOTH:
> COBIT at the governance layer, ITIL at the operational layer.

---

**Q19. ITIL is developed and maintained by which organization?**

A) ISACA
B) ISO/IEC
C) AICPA
D) AXELOS ✅

> **Explanation:** **ITIL is developed by AXELOS** — a joint venture
> originally established by the UK government's Cabinet Office.
> ISACA develops COBIT. ISO/IEC develops international standards.
> AICPA created the SOC framework. Knowing ISACA (COBIT) vs AXELOS
> (ITIL) is directly tested in MCQs.

---

**Q20. The one-line distinction "Are we doing the RIGHT things?" vs
"Are we doing things RIGHT?" maps to COBIT and ITIL in which order?**

A) ITIL = "doing right things" / COBIT = "doing things right"
B) COBIT = "doing right things" / ITIL = "doing things right" ✅
C) Both frameworks ask "doing right things"
D) COBIT = "doing things right" / ITIL = "doing right things"

> **Explanation:** The classic distinction:
> **COBIT = "Are we doing the RIGHT things?"** — governance ensures
> IT is aligned with business strategy.
> **ITIL = "Are we doing things RIGHT?"** — service management ensures
> IT services are delivered efficiently and effectively.
> This exact framing appears very frequently in MCQs.

---

**Q21. Val IT and Risk IT were separate ISACA frameworks that were
merged into which version of COBIT?**

A) COBIT 4.1
B) COBIT 5 ✅
C) COBIT 2019
D) COBIT 3

> **Explanation:** **COBIT 5 (2012) merged Val IT and Risk IT** into a
> single unified framework. Val IT focused on IT value delivery and
> investment management. Risk IT focused on IT-related risk management.
> Bringing them into COBIT 5 created a more comprehensive and integrated
> governance framework.

---

**Q22. ISACA's certification that is MOST directly related to COBIT
and IT governance is:**

A) CISA
B) CISM
C) CRISC
D) CGEIT ✅

> **Explanation:** **CGEIT = Certified in the Governance of Enterprise
> IT** — the ISACA certification most directly aligned with COBIT and
> IT governance. CISA is for IS auditing. CISM is for security
> management. CRISC is for IT risk. CGEIT specifically targets IT
> governance professionals who implement and manage frameworks like
> COBIT.

---

**Q23. COBIT 2019 introduced Design Factors to enable organizations
to do which of the following?**

A) Certify against COBIT as an auditable standard
B) Replace ITIL with COBIT for service management
C) Tailor the COBIT governance system to their specific context
   and needs ✅
D) Measure process capability using a 0–5 scale

> **Explanation:** **Design Factors** were introduced in COBIT 2019
> to allow organizations to **customize and tailor COBIT** to their
> specific context — enterprise strategy, risk profile, compliance
> requirements, IT role, industry sector, etc. This addressed the
> criticism that COBIT 5 was too generic. Capability levels (0–5)
> existed before COBIT 2019.

---

**Q24. In the COBIT capability model (COBIT 5 and 2019), a process
that is planned, monitored, and adjusted — but not yet using a
defined standard process organization-wide — is at which level?**

A) Level 1 — Performed
B) Level 2 — Managed ✅
C) Level 3 — Established
D) Level 4 — Predictable

> **Explanation:** **Level 2 = Managed** — the process is planned,
> monitored, and adjusted, but it is not yet using a standardized
> process across the organization. **Level 3 = Established** is where
> a defined standard process is used organization-wide. Level 1
> (Performed) achieves the process purpose but with no planning or
> monitoring. Level 4 (Predictable) adds quantitative management.

---

**Q25. Which of the following is a COBIT 2019 Focus Area specifically
designed to provide governance guidance for cloud environments?**

A) EDM05
B) Cloud Computing ✅
C) BAI10
D) Digital Architecture

> **Explanation:** **Cloud Computing** is one of the COBIT 2019 Focus
> Areas — providing specialized governance guidance specifically for
> organizations operating in or transitioning to cloud environments.
> Other Focus Areas include Information Security, DevOps, Digital
> Transformation, Privacy, Cybersecurity, Agile, and SME guidance.
> EDM05 and BAI10 are specific governance/management objectives.

**Q26. ISO 38500 and COBIT both address IT governance. The key
difference between them is:**

A) ISO 38500 is for large enterprises only; COBIT is for SMEs
B) ISO 38500 is a high-level principles standard; COBIT is a
   comprehensive practical framework ✅
C) ISO 38500 provides detailed process guidance; COBIT provides
   only principles
D) ISO 38500 is mandatory; COBIT is voluntary

> **Explanation:** **ISO 38500 is a high-level IT governance standard**
> — it provides six principles for the governance of IT but no detailed
> implementation guidance. **COBIT is a comprehensive practical
> framework** — providing detailed processes, objectives, activities,
> and metrics. They address the same domain but at very different levels
> of detail. Both are voluntary.

---

**Q27. COBIT 4.1 used a Maturity Model while COBIT 5 and 2019 use a
Capability Model. A key similarity between both is:**

A) Both use a 1–10 scale
B) Both use a 0–5 scale ✅
C) Both are based on ISO 27001
D) Both require external certification to validate

> **Explanation:** Both the COBIT 4.1 **Maturity Model** and the
> COBIT 5/2019 **Capability Model** use a **0–5 scale**. The names
> of the levels differ slightly, and the underlying methodology
> changed (Capability Model uses ISO/IEC 33000 series). But both
> assess process quality on a 0 (worst) to 5 (best) scale.

**Q28. The COBIT 4.1 domain "Deliver and Support (DS)" had how many
processes and primarily covered which area?**

A) 7 processes — strategy and architecture
B) 10 processes — solutions acquisition
C) 4 processes — performance monitoring
D) 13 processes — service delivery, security, and continuity ✅

> **Explanation:** **Deliver and Support (DS)** was the largest COBIT
> 4.1 domain with **13 processes** — covering service delivery, IT
> security, data management, facilities management, service desk,
> and business continuity. Plan and Organise had 10. Acquire and
> Implement had 7. Monitor and Evaluate had 4.

**Q29. An organization has identified that its current IT governance
is ad hoc and reactive with no formal risk management process. In
COBIT 2019 capability terms, this organization is at:**

A) Level 2 — Managed
B) Level 1 — Performed
C) Level 3 — Established
D) Level 0 — Incomplete ✅

> **Explanation:** **Level 0 = Incomplete** — the process is not
> implemented or completely fails to achieve its purpose. An ad hoc,
> reactive organization with no formal risk management process has
> not even achieved Level 1 (Performed). Level 1 means the process
> at least achieves its basic purpose, even if unplanned.

**Q30. Which combination of COBIT facts is entirely correct?**

A) COBIT by AICPA / COBIT 5 = 4 domains / COBIT 2019 = 37 objectives /
   EDM is management domain
B) COBIT by ISACA / COBIT 4.1 = 34 processes / COBIT 5 = 37 processes /
   EDM is governance domain ✅
C) COBIT by ISACA / COBIT 5 = 5 principles / COBIT 2019 = 5 principles /
   ITIL by ISACA
D) COBIT by ISACA / COBIT 4.1 = 37 processes / COBIT 2019 = 40 objectives /
   APO is governance domain

> **Explanation:** The fully correct combination is:
> **COBIT by ISACA** ✅ (not AICPA)
> **COBIT 4.1 = 34 processes** ✅ (not 37 or 40)
> **COBIT 5 = 37 processes** ✅ (not 40)
> **EDM is the governance domain** ✅ (not management)
> Option C fails because COBIT 2019 has 6 principles (not 5) and
> ITIL is by AXELOS (not ISACA). Option D fails because COBIT 4.1
> has 34 (not 37) processes and APO is management (not governance).
