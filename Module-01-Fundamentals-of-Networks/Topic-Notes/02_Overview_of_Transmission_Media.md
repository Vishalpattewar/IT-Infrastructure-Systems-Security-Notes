# Overview of Transmission Media
## Module: Fundamentals of Networks

---

## 1. Introduction
Transmission media refers to the physical or wireless pathways through which data travels from sender to receiver.

- It acts as the **channel** in a communication system.
- It determines speed, reliability, cost, and distance of communication.

### Why it exists
- To carry signals between devices
- To enable wired and wireless communication
- To support different networking environments

### Where it fits
- It is the **physical foundation of networking**
- Works at the **Physical Layer (Layer 1)** of networking models

---

## 2. Core Concepts & Detailed Explanation

### 2.1 Classification of Transmission Media

Transmission media are divided into two main types:

1. **Guided Media (Wired)**
2. **Unguided Media (Wireless)**

---

### 2.2 Guided (Wired) Media

These use physical cables to transmit signals.

#### A. Twisted Pair Cable

- Two insulated copper wires twisted together
- Reduces electromagnetic interference (EMI)

**Types:**
- UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair)
- STP (Shielded Twisted Pair)

**Categories:**
- Cat5, Cat5e, Cat6, Cat6a, Cat7, Cat8

**Uses:**
- Ethernet networks (LAN)

---

#### B. Coaxial Cable

- Central conductor + insulation + shielding
- Better noise resistance than twisted pair

**Types:**
- RG-6, RG-58, RG-59

**Uses:**
- Cable TV, broadband

---

#### C. Fiber Optic Cable

- Uses light signals instead of electrical signals
- Extremely high speed and long-distance communication

**Types:**
- Single Mode Fiber (SMF)
- Multi Mode Fiber (MMF)

**Advantages:**
- High bandwidth
- Immune to EMI
- Secure

---

### 2.3 Unguided (Wireless) Media

Signals travel through air or vacuum.

#### A. Radio Waves

- Used in Wi-Fi, Bluetooth
- Omni-directional

---

#### B. Microwaves

- Line-of-sight communication
- Used in satellite and long-distance links

---

#### C. Infrared

- Short-range communication
- Requires line-of-sight

---

#### D. Satellite Communication

- Uses orbiting satellites (GEO, MEO, LEO)
- Covers large geographical areas

---

### 2.4 Transmission Impairments

Factors that degrade signal quality:

- **Attenuation** → Signal weakening
- **Distortion** → Signal shape change
- **Noise** → External interference

---

### 2.5 Signal Propagation Effects

- Reflection
- Diffraction
- Scattering

These affect wireless communication quality.

## 3. Key Components / Types / Categories

### Comparison of Transmission Media

| Feature | Twisted Pair | Coaxial | Fiber Optic |
|--------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Cost | Low | Medium | High |
| Speed | Medium | High | Very High |
| Distance | Short | Medium | Long |
| EMI Resistance | Low | Medium | High |

---

### Wired vs Wireless

| Feature | Wired | Wireless |
|--------|------|---------|
| Reliability | High | Medium |
| Mobility | Low | High |
| Security | High | Lower |
| Installation | Complex | Easy |

---

## 4. How It Works — Step by Step

1. Data is converted into signal
2. Signal enters transmission medium
3. Signal travels through medium
4. Signal may be affected by noise/attenuation
5. Signal reaches receiver
6. Signal converted back to data

---

| Bandwidth | Capacity of medium |
| Attenuation | Signal loss over distance |

| Fiber Optics | Light-based transmission |

---

## 6. Key Facts to Remember

1. Transmission media = path for data transmission
2. Two types: Guided and Unguided
3. Fiber optics provide highest speed and security
4. Twisted pair is most commonly used in LAN
5. Wireless offers mobility but less security
6. Attenuation increases with distance
7. EMI affects copper cables but not fiber
8. Satellite communication covers large areas

---

## 7. Common Comparisons

### UTP vs STP

| Feature | UTP | STP |
|--------|----|----|
| Shielding | No | Yes |
| Cost | Low | High |
| EMI Protection | Low | High |

---

### Fiber vs Copper

| Feature | Fiber | Copper |
|--------|------|--------|
| Signal Type | Light | Electrical |
| Speed | Very High | Moderate |
| Interference | None | Present |

---

## 8. Real-World Analogy

Think of transmission media like roads:

- Twisted pair → City roads (short distance, cheap)
- Coaxial → Highways (better performance)
- Fiber optic → Expressways (fastest, long-distance)
- Wireless → Air travel (no physical road, flexible)

---

## 9. Quick Summary

- Transmission media carries data signals
- Two main types: wired and wireless
- Fiber optics offer best performance
- Wireless provides flexibility
- Signal quality affected by noise and attenuation
- Choice depends on cost, speed, and distance
- Foundation of physical networking
