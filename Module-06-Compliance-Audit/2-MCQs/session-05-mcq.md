## MCQ Set

---

**Q1. ISO stands for and is headquartered in which city?**

A) International Standards Organization — Brussels, Belgium
B) International Organization for Standardization — Geneva, Switzerland ✅
C) International Organization for Standardization — New York, USA
D) International Security Organization — London, UK

> **Explanation:** **ISO = International Organization for Standardization**
> and its headquarters is in **Geneva, Switzerland**. The name ISO comes
> from the Greek word "isos" meaning equal — chosen so the acronym would
> be consistent across all languages. It is NOT an acronym derived from
> the English name.

---

**Q2. In the ISO/IEC 2700x family, which is the ONLY certifiable standard?**

A) ISO/IEC 27002
B) ISO/IEC 27005
C) ISO/IEC 27001 ✅
D) ISO/IEC 27000

> **Explanation:** **ISO/IEC 27001 is the only certifiable standard**
> in the entire 2700x family. ISO 27002 provides guidance for implementing
> controls. ISO 27005 covers risk management. ISO 27000 provides vocabulary
> and definitions. None of the others can be certified against — only
> 27001 supports third-party certification.

---

**Q3. ISO/IEC 27002 relates to ISO/IEC 27001 in which way?**

A) 27002 contains the ISMS requirements; 27001 provides implementation
   guidance
B) 27002 is the certifiable standard; 27001 is the guidance document
C) 27001 contains ISMS requirements; 27002 provides guidance for
   implementing the controls ✅
D) 27001 and 27002 are identical standards with different names

> **Explanation:** **27001 = requirements (WHAT to do)** and **27002 =
> guidance (HOW to do it)**. You get certified to 27001. You USE 27002
> as a detailed guide for implementing each Annex A control. Think of
> 27001 as the law and 27002 as the study guide. A common MCQ trap is
> swapping their roles.

---

**Q4. ISO/IEC 27701 extends ISO/IEC 27001 specifically to cover:**

A) Cloud security controls for service providers
B) Privacy information management — helping demonstrate GDPR compliance ✅
C) ICT readiness for business continuity
D) Information security metrics and measurement

> **Explanation:** **ISO/IEC 27701** extends 27001 to cover **Privacy
> Information Management** — it maps directly to GDPR requirements and
> helps organizations demonstrate privacy compliance. ISO 27017 covers
> cloud security. ISO 27031 covers ICT readiness for BCM. ISO 27004
> covers metrics and measurement.

---

**Q5. The British standard that directly preceded and evolved into
ISO/IEC 27001 was:**

A) BS 7800
B) BS 7799 ✅
C) BS 8800
D) BS 7700

> **Explanation:** **BS 7799** published by the **British Standards
> Institution (BSI)** is the direct predecessor of ISO 27001.
> BS 7799 Part 1 became ISO 17799 (later renumbered ISO 27002).
> BS 7799 Part 2 became ISO 27001. The BS 7799 name and its connection
> to ISO 27001 is a very common MCQ question.

---

**Q6. In which year was the first ISO/IEC 27001 standard published,
replacing BS 7799 Part 2?**

A) 2000
B) 2002
C) 2003
D) 2005 ✅

> **Explanation:** **ISO/IEC 27001:2005** was the first published ISO
> version of the ISMS standard — published in 2005. Before this, BS 7799
> Part 2 (2002) was the certifiable standard. The 2005 version organized
> controls into 11 domains with 133 controls.

---

**Q7. ISO/IEC 27001:2005 organized its Annex A controls into how many
domains with how many controls?**

A) 10 domains — 127 controls
B) 11 domains — 133 controls ✅
C) 12 domains — 140 controls
D) 14 domains — 114 controls

> **Explanation:** **ISO 27001:2005 = 11 domains, 133 controls.**
> The 2013 version has 14 domains and 114 controls. The 2022 version
> has 4 themes and 93 controls. These three version-to-number mappings
> are the most tested facts in this entire session.

---

**Q8. Which of the following was added as a NEW standalone domain in
ISO/IEC 27001:2013 that did NOT exist as a separate domain in the 2005
version?**

A) Access Control
B) Asset Management
C) Cryptography ✅
D) Physical and Environmental Security

> **Explanation:** **Cryptography (A.10)** was added as a new standalone
> domain in the 2013 version — it did not exist as a separate domain in
> 2005 (cryptographic controls were part of IS Acquisition, Development
> & Maintenance). **Supplier Relationships (A.15)** was also a new
> standalone domain in 2013. Access Control, Asset Management, and
> Physical Security existed in both versions.

---

**Q9. ISO/IEC 27001:2013 organized Annex A into how many domains
containing how many controls?**

A) 11 domains — 133 controls
B) 12 domains — 120 controls
C) 14 domains — 114 controls ✅
D) 15 domains — 130 controls

> **Explanation:** **ISO 27001:2013 = 14 domains, 114 controls.**
> This is one of the three critical version facts: 2005 (11/133) →
> 2013 (14/114) → 2022 (4 themes/93). The 2013 version reduced the
> number of controls from 133 to 114 while expanding domains from
> 11 to 14 by splitting and reorganizing existing domains.

---

**Q10. ISO/IEC 27001:2022 restructured Annex A controls into which
format?**

A) 12 domains with 110 controls
B) 14 domains with 93 controls
C) 5 themes with 100 controls
D) 4 themes with 93 controls ✅

> **Explanation:** **ISO 27001:2022 = 4 themes, 93 controls.** The
> four themes are: Organizational Controls (37), People Controls (8),
> Physical Controls (14), and Technological Controls (34). This was
> a major restructuring from the 14-domain model of the 2013 version.
> Published in October 2022.

---

**Q11. In ISO/IEC 27001:2022, which theme contains the highest number
of controls (37 controls)?**

A) People Controls
B) Physical Controls
C) Technological Controls
D) Organizational Controls ✅

> **Explanation:** In ISO 27001:2022 Annex A, **Organizational Controls
> (Theme 5)** contains the most controls with **37**. Technological
> Controls (Theme 8) has 34. Physical Controls (Theme 7) has 14.
> People Controls (Theme 6) has the fewest with 8.

---

**Q12. Which of the following correctly lists ALL 4 themes of
ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A?**

A) Organizational, Technical, Human, Environmental
B) Governance, People, Physical, Technology
C) Organizational, People, Physical, Technological ✅
D) Management, Operational, Physical, Digital

> **Explanation:** The exact four themes in ISO 27001:2022 are:
> **Organizational Controls, People Controls, Physical Controls, and
> Technological Controls.** The correct terms are "Organizational"
> not "Governance" and "Technological" not "Technology" or "Technical."
> Exact terminology matters in MCQs.

---

**Q13. Which ISO 27001 clause requires organizations to define and
document the scope of their ISMS?**

A) Clause 5.2
B) Clause 6.1.3
C) Clause 4.3 ✅
D) Clause 8.1

> **Explanation:** **Clause 4.3** specifically requires organizations
> to determine the **scope of the ISMS** — documenting the boundaries
> of what is included. This is critical because organizations can
> choose to certify only a part of their operations. Clause 5.2 covers
> security policy. Clause 6.1.3 covers risk treatment and SoA.

---

**Q14. Which ISO 27001 clause specifically requires the production of
a Statement of Applicability (SoA)?**

A) Clause 4.2
B) Clause 5.1
C) Clause 6.1.3 ✅
D) Clause 9.2

> **Explanation:** **Clause 6.1.3** — Information Security Risk
> Treatment — requires organizations to produce a **Statement of
> Applicability (SoA)**. The SoA lists all Annex A controls with
> their inclusion/exclusion status and justification. It is one of
> the most important mandatory documents in any ISO 27001 ISMS.

---

**Q15. The Statement of Applicability (SoA) in ISO 27001 primarily
serves as:**

A) The organization's information security policy document
B) The bridge between risk assessment results and selected Annex A
   controls ✅
C) The formal certification document issued by the certification body
D) The internal audit report produced during Clause 9.2

> **Explanation:** The **SoA bridges risk assessment and control
> selection** — for every Annex A control it states: included or
> excluded, why, and implementation status. It connects the risks
> found (Clause 6.1.2) to the controls chosen (Annex A). Auditors
> review it to verify that risk treatment decisions are rational and
> documented.

---

**Q16. ISO 27001 requires top management to demonstrate commitment to
the ISMS under which clause?**

A) Clause 4.1
B) Clause 5.1 ✅
C) Clause 6.2
D) Clause 8.1

> **Explanation:** **Clause 5 — Leadership** — specifically **Clause
> 5.1** requires top management to demonstrate commitment to the ISMS.
> ISO 27001 explicitly requires that security is NOT just an IT
> department function — it must have visible leadership commitment
> at the highest level. Without this, certification will fail.

---

**Q17. In the PDCA cycle as applied to ISO 27001, which clauses map
to the "CHECK" phase?**

A) Clauses 4, 5, 6, 7
B) Clause 8
C) Clause 9 ✅
D) Clause 10

> **Explanation:** The PDCA mapping for ISO 27001 is:
> **Plan = Clauses 4,5,6,7** (establish ISMS)
> **Do = Clause 8** (implement ISMS)
> **Check = Clause 9** (monitor, measure, internal audit, management
> review)
> **Act = Clause 10** (improve and correct)
> Clause 9 = CHECK because it covers monitoring, measurement, and
> evaluation activities.

---

**Q18. Which phase of the PDCA cycle maps to Clause 10 —
Improvement in ISO 27001?**

A) Plan
B) Do
C) Check
D) Act ✅

> **Explanation:** **Clause 10 = Act** — it covers continual
> improvement and corrective actions (addressing nonconformities).
> Act is the "close the loop" phase — using check results to drive
> improvements. Plan=4-7, Do=8, Check=9, Act=10.

---

**Q19. ISO 27001 internal audits are covered under which clause and
serve what primary purpose?**

A) Clause 8.2 — implement risk treatment
B) Clause 9.2 — verify ISMS is effectively implemented and maintained ✅
C) Clause 6.1.2 — conduct risk assessments
D) Clause 10.2 — address nonconformities

> **Explanation:** **Clause 9.2** covers **Internal Audit** — conducted
> at planned intervals to verify the ISMS conforms to requirements and
> is effectively implemented. Internal audits feed results into
> **management review (Clause 9.3)** which then drives improvements
> in **Clause 10**. Clause 8.2 covers risk assessments during
> operations.

---

**Q20. Which of the following is NOT one of the four risk treatment
options under ISO 27001?**

A) Modify (Mitigate)
B) Accept
C) Transfer (Share)
D) Escalate ✅

> **Explanation:** The **four risk treatment options** are: **Modify
> (Mitigate), Accept, Avoid, and Transfer (Share).** "Escalate" is
> not a risk treatment option in ISO 27001. Escalation is a process
> within incident management — not a risk treatment strategy.

---

**Q21. An organization identifies a risk but decides the cost of
mitigation far exceeds the potential loss. They formally document
this decision and do nothing else. Which risk treatment option are
they applying?**

A) Avoid
B) Transfer
C) Modify
D) Accept ✅

> **Explanation:** **Accept** = consciously acknowledging the risk
> exists and formally deciding to live with it — typically when
> cost of mitigation exceeds potential impact. The key requirement
> is that the acceptance must be **documented and formally approved**
> — it cannot be accidental or undocumented. This is valid under
> ISO 27001.

---

**Q22. An organization stops using an insecure legacy system entirely
rather than trying to secure it. Which risk treatment option is this?**

A) Accept
B) Transfer
C) Avoid ✅
D) Modify

> **Explanation:** **Avoid** = eliminating the risk by stopping the
> activity that creates it. Shutting down a system to eliminate
> its associated risks is a classic example of risk avoidance.
> Modify would mean implementing controls on the legacy system.
> Transfer would mean outsourcing its management.

---

**Q23. ISO 27001 certification is conducted by:**

A) ISO directly — after reviewing the organization's documentation
B) NIST — as the international certification authority
C) Accredited third-party Certification Bodies ✅
D) The organization's internal audit team

> **Explanation:** **ISO does NOT certify organizations** — this is one
> of the most common MCQ traps in this session. **Accredited
> third-party Certification Bodies** conduct Stage 1 and Stage 2
> audits and issue certificates. ISO only publishes the standard.
> Internal audits (Clause 9.2) support the ISMS but cannot result
> in certification.

---

**Q24. The ISO 27001 certification audit consists of two stages.
Stage 1 audit primarily involves:**

A) On-site testing of whether security controls are actually working
B) Review of the organization's ISMS documentation for readiness ✅
C) Penetration testing of the organization's systems
D) Interviewing all employees about security awareness

> **Explanation:** **Stage 1 = Documentation Review** — the
> certification body reviews the ISMS documentation (policies,
> risk assessments, SoA, procedures) to check readiness for the
> full audit. **Stage 2 = Certification Audit** — on-site
> evaluation of whether controls are actually implemented and
> working. Stage 1 findings must be addressed before Stage 2
> proceeds.

---

**Q25. An ISO 27001 certificate is valid for how many years, and
what happens during that period?**

A) 1 year — full recertification audit every year
B) 2 years — one surveillance audit at year 1
C) 3 years — annual surveillance audits at years 1 and 2 ✅
D) 5 years — one surveillance audit at year 3

> **Explanation:** **ISO 27001 certificate = valid for 3 years.**
> During the 3-year cycle: **Year 1 and Year 2 = annual surveillance
> audits** (smaller scope, verifying ISMS continues to function).
> **Year 3 = full recertification audit** to renew the certificate
> for another 3 years. Annual surveillance audits are mandatory —
> missing one can result in certificate suspension.

---

**Q26. Which of the following correctly maps the ISO/IEC 27001:2005
domain "A.10" to its subject?**

A) Access Control
B) Asset Management
C) Communications and Operations Management ✅
D) Information Security Incident Management

> **Explanation:** In the **2005 version**, **A.10 = Communications
> and Operations Management** — covering operational procedures,
> malware controls, backup, network monitoring, and media handling.
> In the **2013 version**, A.10 was reassigned to Cryptography.
> This version-to-domain mapping is a classic MCQ trap — the same
> clause number means different things in different versions.

---

**Q27. In ISO/IEC 27001:2013, which domain (A.15) covers management
of risks arising from third-party vendors and suppliers?**

A) System Acquisition, Development and Maintenance
B) Compliance
C) Information Security Incident Management
D) Supplier Relationships ✅

> **Explanation:** **A.15 = Supplier Relationships** in the 2013
> version — a NEW domain added in 2013 that did not exist as a
> standalone domain in 2005. It covers information security policies
> for supplier relationships, addressing risks from third-party
> vendors, cloud providers, and outsourced services.

---

**Q28. ISO/IEC 27001 is best described as which type of standard?**

A) A prescriptive technical standard specifying exact controls to
   implement
B) A technology-neutral, risk-based management system standard
   applicable to any organization ✅
C) A compliance checklist for financial institutions only
D) A government-mandated security standard for critical infrastructure

> **Explanation:** ISO 27001 is **technology-neutral and sector-neutral**
> — it does NOT specify which exact controls must be implemented. Control
> selection is based on the organization's own **risk assessment**. It
> applies to any organization of any size, sector, or country. It is
> voluntary globally — not government-mandated.

---

**Q29. Which ISO/IEC 2700x standard specifically provides vocabulary
and definitions used across the entire 2700x family?**

A) ISO/IEC 27001
B) ISO/IEC 27002
C) ISO/IEC 27000 ✅
D) ISO/IEC 27003

> **Explanation:** **ISO/IEC 27000** is the overview and vocabulary
> standard — it provides all definitions and terminology used across
> the entire 2700x family. ISO 27001 Clause 3 (Terms and Definitions)
> explicitly references ISO/IEC 27000. ISO 27002 = control guidance.
> ISO 27003 = ISMS implementation guidance.

---

**Q30. Which combination of ISO 27001 facts is entirely correct?**

A) 2005: 14 domains / 2013: 11 domains / 2022: 4 themes —
   Certification by ISO directly — SoA required by Clause 5.2
B) 2005: 11 domains / 2013: 14 domains / 2022: 4 themes —
   Certification by accredited bodies — SoA required by Clause 6.1.3 ✅
C) 2005: 11 domains / 2013: 14 domains / 2022: 6 themes —
   Certification by ISO directly — SoA required by Clause 9.2
D) 2005: 12 domains / 2013: 14 domains / 2022: 4 themes —
   Certification by accredited bodies — SoA required by Clause 8.1

> **Explanation:** The fully correct combination is:
> **2005 = 11 domains** ✅
> **2013 = 14 domains** ✅
> **2022 = 4 themes** ✅
> **Certification = accredited third-party bodies (not ISO)** ✅
> **SoA = required by Clause 6.1.3** ✅
> This question tests whether all five key facts are known together —
> a common style for final MCQ sets.
